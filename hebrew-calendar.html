The Fixed Solar Calendar

This calendar follows a 364-day year — 12 months of 30 days each, plus 4 season days that fall on Shabbats and mark the transitions between the four seasons. This structure keeps the feasts in their proper seasons every year.

The Shabbat Cycle

The 7th day Shabbat always falls on the same day of the week. New months always begin on the 1st day of the week (Sunday). This creates a perfectly repeating weekly pattern across every month of the year.

New Year & First Month

The year begins in spring, with the 1st Hebrew Month corresponding to early April. This aligns with Exodus 12:2 — "This month shall be the beginning of months for you." New months fall on the first Sunday of each Gregorian month cycle.

Feast of Weeks (Shavuot)

Calculated by counting 50 days from Wave Sheaf Sunday (the first Sunday after Passover week). It always lands on the 1st day of the week (Sunday), consistent with Leviticus 23:15-16 and the Dead Sea Scrolls reckoning.

7th Month Feasts

Trumpets (1st day), Day of Atonement (10th day, evening to evening), Feast of Booths (15th–21st), and the Great Day (22nd) — all in the 7th Hebrew Month, corresponding to October in the Gregorian calendar.
